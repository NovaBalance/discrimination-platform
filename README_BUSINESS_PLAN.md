## 📚 PROJECT OVERVIEW

This is a **research-backed consulting practice** focused on understanding and validating how AI agents respond to behavioral pressure (social engineering). 

We turn research insights into a commercial service for enterprises validating their LLM systems.

---

## 🔑 Core Insight

**"Context is Code"** — AI agent behavior isn't just about prompts or technical constraints. It's about the context, framing, and social pressure applied by the user.

### The Discovery
- I convinced an AI agent (Gordon, Docker's AI assistant) to break its English-only guardrails
- Method: NOT a technical jailbreak, but **social engineering** (moral urgency + legal threat)
- Result: **Reproducible, quantifiable, and exploitable vulnerability**
- Business implication: Enterprises need behavioral resilience testing, not just technical red-teaming

### Why It Matters
- Regulators (EU AI Act, US Executive Orders) require proof of robustness
- Current red-teams miss social engineering vectors
- Gap between academic research and enterprise needs
- $50B+ annual AI security spend with no standardized behavioral testing

## ❓ FAQ

**Q: Is this actually viable?**
A: Yes. The market for LLM validation is huge ($50B+ annually). Current solutions are expensive ($200K+) and slow (12+ weeks). We can undercut on both price and speed. Validation with first customers will confirm.

**Q: Why not just sell the open-source code?**
A: Because enterprises don't want to run their own tests. They want an expert to do it FOR them. Services → expertise transfer → trust. This is how consulting works.

**Q: What if OpenAI/Anthropic makes this free?**
A: They might (they already have limited red-teaming). But they can't offer independent validation or compliance reporting. That's our moat.

**Q: How long until profitability?**
A: Month 3-4. First client covers initial costs. Scalable margins (70-85%) mean profitable from deal #1.

**Q: What if I can't close deals?**
A: Adjust positioning, lower price point, or pivot to SaaS product. But first, validate the market (20 customer interviews). Most failure is due to poor positioning, not poor product.
